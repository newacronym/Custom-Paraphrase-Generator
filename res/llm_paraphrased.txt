A cover letter is a formal document that accompanies your resume. It serves as an introduction for your application. The primary purpose of a cover letter is to introduce yourself to the hiring manager and to provide context for your resume. You can elaborate on your qualifications, skills, and experiences in a way that your resume may not capture. It's also an opportunity to explain why you would be a good fit for the role and the company. The following sections are included in a typical cover letter. Includes your contact information, the date and the employer's contact information. 2. The greeting should be personalized with the hiring manager's name. 3. Introduce yourself and the position you're applying for. 4. The core of your cover letter is your qualifications, experiences, and skills that make you suitable for the job. You can talk about how you can help the company. 5. Thoroughly summarizes your points and reiterates your enthusiasm for the role. Asking for an interview can be included in a call to action. 6. "sincerely," "best regards," etc. followed by your name. The cover letter is the first document that a hiring manager will read, so it sets the tone for your entire application. It gives you a chance to stand out and make a good first impression. Failing to include a cover letter could result in your application being ignored by an employer. A cover letter is an essential component of a job application that serves to introduce you, elaborate on your qualifications, and make a compelling case for why you should be considered for the position.
